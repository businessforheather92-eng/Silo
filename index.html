Why these tools

Built on the research, not productivity folklore.

Every tool in Silo maps to something the ADHD literature actually supports — and where the evidence is early, the app says so right on the label instead of overselling it.

Well studied

Body doubling

Working alongside another presence measurably helps ADHD task initiation and follow-through. The AI companion gives you that presence on demand — face, voice, and quiet company.

Well studied

Noise & attention

Stochastic-resonance studies show steady moderate noise improves focus and working memory in ADHD, under-stimulated brains. The Sound tool is organized by goal, with an honest evidence label on every option.

Well studied

Task chunking

ADHD task paralysis responds to shrinking the first step below the activation threshold. The AI chunker turns "do taxes" into a step small enough to actually start.

Well studied

Implementation intentions

"I will do X at Y" beats willpower in executive-function research. Intention, Routines, and the session launcher are all built around this one finding.

Working memory

External capture

ADHD working memory drops thoughts mid-flight. A zero-friction capture box — one hotkey from anywhere — moves the thought out of your head before it's gone.

Self-regulation

Interoception & breaks

Hyperfocus skips hunger, thirst and posture until the crash. The circuit breaker and body check bring the body back into the loop, gently.

Questions

Fair questions.

Is the free tier actually usable, or a demo?

It's a real daily loop, no account needed: quick capture, the focus timer with progress bars, the task list, emotion/intention check-ins, the three classic sounds (white, brown, rain), and a body-double companion in quiet-company mode. Pro — $24.99 once, or $7.99/mo after a 14-day free trial — unlocks everything else at once: the remaining tools, the full sound library, your companion's live voice, and every AI feature. Never sold per-tool.

How does the free trial work?

Start the Monthly plan and you get 14 days of full Pro access before your card is charged. Cancel any time during the trial and you won't be billed. If you keep it, billing starts automatically at $7.99/mo on day 15 — no separate step needed.

Lifetime or monthly — which should I pick?

They unlock exactly the same things. Lifetime pays for itself in about three months of the monthly price and then owes nothing ever again — that's the better deal for almost everyone. Monthly starts with a 14-day free trial if you'd rather try Pro before committing, or just prefer paying as you go. You can start on monthly and switch to lifetime later; email us and we'll sort it out.

Where does my data live?

In your browser's local storage, on your device. There's no server holding your tasks, no account, and nothing to breach. The ⛃ Data button exports a complete backup file whenever you want one — keep it somewhere safe, because clearing your browser data clears the app too.

What exactly does the AI do?

Four things: chunks intimidating tasks into startable steps, helps you recover a thought you lost mid-sentence, sorts messy brain dumps into to-dos / reminders / feelings, and powers the body-double companion that keeps you company while you work. Messages go through our server to Anthropic's Claude and aren't stored.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes — it's a responsive web app, and you can install it to your home screen (your browser will offer, or use "Add to Home Screen") so it opens like a native app, offline included.

What if I buy Pro and hate it?

Bought Lifetime? Email us within 14 days for a full refund, no questions, no guilt-trip. On Monthly, just cancel — no charge hits you again, and you keep access until the period you paid for ends. Your Pro account signs in from any browser or device — it unlocks the Pro tools and AI, while your tasks and notes never leave your own browser.

Who made this?

An ADHD household that got tired of productivity apps designed for brains that show up the same every day. This one is built around the bad days, not just the good ones — and around what the research actually supports.
